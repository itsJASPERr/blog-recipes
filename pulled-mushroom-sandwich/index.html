Pulled Mushroom Sandwich

Posted by Jasper Meyer on 2019-09-09

Ingredients

  • 250g Oyster Mushrooms (Austernpilze)
  • 250g King Oyster Mushrooms (Kräuterseitlinge)
  • 100g pineapple
  • 2 red onions
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 5 tbs agave sirup
  • 5 tbs maple sirup
  • 4 tbs olive oil
  • 1 tbs cumin
  • 1 tbs parika (spicy)
  • 2 tbs soy sauce
  • 1 tsp cayenne
  • 2 tsp tumeric
  • salt
  • pepper
  • 1 lime
  • 1 tsp liquid smoke (optional)
  • 1 hand full fresh cilantro (optional)

Instructions

  • Use a fork to "pull" the mushrooms into stripes as much as possible. Cut the rest and the heads in stripes. And add to a large bowl.
  • Halve the onions and cut them sideways into thin slices. Add them to the mushrooms.
  • Add the spices, soy sauce, 1-2 tbs olive oil, 1 tbs agave, 1 tbs maple sirup, and liqiud smoke. Mix well and let that marinate for a bit.
  • Meanwhile, blend the pinapple into a purree (you could add roasted red bell pepper if you fancy)
  • Heat up a non-sticky pan with 2 tbs oil.
  • Add the mushrroms mix and roast on medium heat. Make sure to not stir too much, rather let them sit on one side for a couple of minutes, then stir. This will create some crunchy parts. Over the time, keep adding the rest of agave and maple sirup.
  • After about 6 minutes Add the pineapple purree and chopped garlic
  • Cook to your preferred consistency (probable something between 12 and 25 minutes)
  • Move the mixture to a serving bowl, add juice of half a lime, salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: mix in chopped cilantro

Making the sandwich

  • ciabatta
  • roma salad
  • cherry tomatoes
  • avocado
  • veganaise
  • red cabbage (optional)

Make some kind of bag out of the ciabatta and smear vegenaise and some hot sauce and some of the pineapple purree into it. Fill it with the pulled mushrooms. Slice the avocado and squeeze them in, as well as some roma salad and tomatoes. You could add some red cabbage as well (fresh or cooked).
